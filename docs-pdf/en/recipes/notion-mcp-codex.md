# Codex × Notion MCP: Open up your knowledge workspace

**Notion** is more than just a note-taking software - it has completed its transformation in the AI ​​era and become a true AI Native product. With Notion MCP, you can let Codex read, summarize, and write directly to your Notion workspace for true AI-driven knowledge management.

---

## 1. Installation

Open the Codex plugin market, search for **Notion**, and click to install.

![image-20260513114556044](../images/image-20260513114556044.png)

Notion has many pertasks involved. When installing, it will jump to the browser. You need to log in to your Notion account and complete authorization so that Codex can access your workspace through MCP:

1. Follow the prompts to complete manual authorization
2. After successful authorization, the system will prompt you to return to Codex
3. Codex automatically completes configuration

After the setting is completed, just use `@` to call the Notion plugin.

![image-20260513114704338](../images/image-20260513114704338.png)

---

## 2. How to use

### Read workspace contents

First let Codex call Notion MCP to verify whether it can read the contents of your workspace normally. A successful return indicates that there is no problem with the connection:

![image-20260513114814177](../images/image-20260513114814177.png)

### Read other workspaces

If you join someone else's workspace, as long as the other party provides the corresponding API document, give the document to Codex and let it complete the connection operation.

The picture below is to let Codex view the content updated yesterday in a workspace I joined. Within the scope of authorization, the reading is completely normal:

![image-20260513115012265](../images/image-20260513115012265.png)

### Summary and writing

After reading the content, you can let Codex summarize it directly. If a good idea comes up during the conversation, you can also let it be written directly to your Notion workspace. After completion, a successful link will be returned:

![image-20260513115116664](../images/image-20260513115116664.png)

Click the link to jump to Notion to view the writing results. The content is completely normal:

![image-20260513115207197](../images/image-20260513115207197.png)
