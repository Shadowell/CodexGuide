# Codex × Figma MCP: Let AI understand your design draft

**Figma MCP** allows Codex to directly read design drafts, screenshot analysis nodes, generate pages, modify components, and draw flow charts. After installation, you only need to specify the design requirements in natural language, and Codex will do the rest.

---

## 1. Installation

Search for **Figma** in the plugin market of Codex Desktop App and click to install.

After installation, you will be redirected to the browser page to complete authorization (the process is similar to Notion MCP, just follow the prompts).

![image-20260513113657970](../images/image-20260513113657970.png)

---

## 2. How to use

Create a new dialog, call Figma MCP with the `@` notation, and describe your needs directly.

**Example 1: Generate flow chart**

Let it generate a user login flowchart with basic steps:

![image-20260513113805456](../images/image-20260513113805456.png)

Click the returned link to view the generated login registration flow chart effect in the browser:

![image-20260513113826886](../images/image-20260513113826886.png)

**Example 2: Generating high-fidelity UI design drawings**

If you feel that the flow chart is too technical, you can ask it to design a product functional architecture diagram, or generate a poster-style high-fidelity UI. Just send the prompt word directly:

![image-20260513113903404](../images/image-20260513113903404.png)

Click the link to view the effect. The overall presentation is very good:

## Summarize

Figma MCP is especially suitable for friends with design needs. By installing this plugin in Codex, you can use conversational methods to accomplish things that previously required professional design skills.

When the threshold for drawing is lowered by AI, the abilities we really need to cultivate are:

1. **Product Thinking** — Know what to do
2. **Business Understanding** — Know why you do what you do
3. **Aesthetic Standards** — Know what is good

Let Codex be the executor, use Figma MCP to amplify your ideas and design your own works.
